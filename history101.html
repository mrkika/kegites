History & Membership

Our Origin

The Kegites Club is the most popular socio-cultural club in Nigeria. The Club was founded in 1962 at the University of Ife (now Obafemi Awolowo University) by visionary students to promote African heritage and unity.


The club had its first Chief in the person of Late Prof. Olusegun Adesina. Since the formation of the club, it has undergone various kinds of transformation, for instance; the Club had its first Female Member in 1968 in the person of L.S.F Dupe Ajayi and in 1974 the Club spread to other institutions of higher learning across Nigeria with Ilya du Tractor (Federal College of Agriculture, Akure, Ondo State, Nigeria) as the first Ilya to get a Keg of Office (Accreditation) from the World Headquarters, Ile Ife.
From inception, Kegites Club has remained one Supreme Club with branches (Ilyasis) in almost all institutions of higher learning both in Nigeria and other parts of the world. As at this day, there are over 100 Ilyasis in Nigeria and Overseas. Ilyasis outside Nigeria include (but not limited to) United Kingdom (Ilya du Queen), Chicago, USA (Ilya du Windy), India (Ilya du Punjab) and more.

Purpose and Growth

The club fosters African traditions, camaraderie, and excellence. It has grown to many Nigerian universities and beyond.

Common Misconceptions

Some wrongly associate the club with secret societies or cultism. In truth, the Kegites Club is cultural and social, emphasizing unity and integrity.

Notable Members

Many distinguished Nigerians have been and are still members, including prominent academics, politicians, and cultural leaders who embody the club's values. Late Obafemi Awolowo was the Eternal Grand Patron of the Club and was succeeded by Ex-President Olusegun Obasanjo. Chief Tele Ikwu a former Deputy Governor of Rivers State is a Chief of RSUST, Professor Wole Soyinka is a founding member, Dr Orji Uzor Kalu is a Patron and the List goes on and on.

Our Motto still remaineth: Unity In Diversity
